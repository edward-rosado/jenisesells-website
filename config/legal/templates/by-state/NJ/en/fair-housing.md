## Source of Lawful Income Protections

New Jersey law expressly prohibits discrimination based on **source of lawful income**. This means we do not refuse to work with, or apply different terms or conditions to, buyers or tenants who use Housing Choice Vouchers (formerly Section 8), government assistance programs, Social Security income, disability payments, or any other lawful source of income.

**We accept all lawful sources of income.** If you or someone you know has been denied housing or real estate services based on their source of income, this may be an illegal act.

## New Jersey Law Against Discrimination (NJLAD)

The NJLAD provides broader protections than federal law and covers housing, employment, and places of public accommodation. Any person who believes they have been discriminated against in violation of the NJLAD may file a complaint with:

- **New Jersey Division on Civil Rights:** 609-292-4605 | www.njcivilrights.gov
- **U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD):** 1-800-669-9777 | www.hud.gov/fairhousing
- **New Jersey Coalition Against Discrimination in Housing (NJCADH)**

## Federal Fair Housing Act

The Federal Fair Housing Act prohibits discrimination in housing based on race, color, national origin, religion, sex, familial status, and disability.
